## Topic
Construct an NCD Vulnerability Index from health-behavior variables in BDHS 2017-18, then test its association with hypertension via multilevel logistic regression. Goal: journal submission (unsubmitted as of 2026-05-15).

## Data
- **Source:** Bangladesh Demographic and Health Survey (BDHS) 2017-18, USAID. PR7 recode file with cluster-level weights (level-1 `wt1`, level-2 `wt2` produced by Stata `.do`).
- **Path:** `Datasets/Data_with_level_wgt_PR7/PR7_with_level_wgt.dta`
- **Sample after filtering:** N = 12,650 (9,084 non-hypertensive, 3,566 hypertensive) as observed via the current pipeline post-Phase-1 expansion. Manuscript-era number was 12,458 — discrepancy likely from a downstream `drop_na()` no longer in force; verify in Phase 6/7.
- **2017-18 chosen over 2022** because (per manuscript) it has more detailed NCD module. `LITERATURE_GAPS.md` §8 confirmed BDHS 2017-18 in fact *lacks* the canonical behavioral risk variables (tobacco/diet/PA/alcohol/family-history) — only structural/environmental additions are feasible from PR7. BDHS 2022 supports the core variables on a larger biomarker subsample; full audit in `LITERATURE_GAPS.md` §4.

## Outcome
`hyper` = 1 if SBP ≥ 140 OR DBP ≥ 90 OR on BP medication (`sb318a`). Built from three BP readings averaged.

## Index inputs (MCA → 5 tiers) — Phase 5 finalised set (2026-05-16, 10 inputs)
Clinical-advice + anthropometry/occupation (original 6):
1. `occupation` — recoded from `sb308` into {Labor-taxing, Not labor-taxing, Not working}
2. `BMI_cat` — WHO 6-class from `sbbm`
3. `salt_intake` — advised to reduce salt (`sb318b`)
4. `lose_wgt` — advised to lose weight (`sb318c`)
5. `stop_smok` — advised to stop smoking (`sb318d`)
6. `exer_more` — advised to exercise more (`sb318e`)

Structural/household-environmental (4 inputs after Phase 5 dropped `cook_location`):
7. `cook_fuel` — `hv226`: clean (electricity/LPG/natural gas/biogas) vs solid/polluting
8. `water_source` — `hv201`: piped/treated vs tubewell-protected vs unimproved/surface
9. `toilet_type` — `hv205`: JMP-improved vs unimproved
10. `crowding` — derived `hv009 / hv216 > 3` per WHO (binary)

`cook_location` was dropped 2026-05-16 after Phase 5 robustness: with 97% of respondents in one category it carried no MCA signal; removing it left the Tier-5 OR essentially unchanged and marginally improved CV AUC.

The MCA input set is exposed as the named vector `ncdvi_inputs` at the top of `R/mca_index.R`. MCA produces 1 dimension → min-max standardized → quintile-cut into Tier 1–5 via `statar::xtile`. `mca_index.R` now also runs an auto-orient step (Tier 5 = highest HTN prevalence), matching the sensitivity index — added after the dim-1 sign flipped silently when cook_location was removed.

The sensitivity index (`ncdvi_se`, structural-only, non-endogenous) is correspondingly 6 inputs (was 7): occupation + BMI_cat + cook_fuel + water_source + toilet_type + crowding.

## Confounders in the multilevel model
diabetic (glucose ≥ 7 mmol/L or on med), age_cat (6 bands), sex, educ (4), marital_status, wealth_index (5), division (8), area_res. Random intercept on `hv021` (PSU). **Primary spec: `lme4::glmer` with `weights = sampling_wgt`** (literature-matched, decided 2026-05-16 after Phases 3b–3d). WeMix::mix with PSU-scaled `wt1` + raw `wt2` reported as Supplementary sensitivity. For the discrimination subsection (Phase 4c M6), the spec replaces `diabetic` and `age_cat` with continuous `glucose` and `age`, adds continuous `BMI`, two interactions (age:sex, sex:BMI), and natural splines on age and BMI.

## Headline result (Phase 3 + Phase 5 final spec, 2026-05-16)
**Primary spec: `lme4::glmer` with sample weights as frequency weights, on the 10-input NCDVI** (after Phase 5 dropped `cook_location`). This is the convention in the BDHS HTN literature. ICC lands in the published 0.05–0.15 range. Direct comparability with competing literature.

**Primary 10-input NCDVI (glmer):** Tier 5 OR = **15.79 (13.12–19.00)**, σ²_u = 0.137, ICC = 0.040. Monotonic dose-response.
**Sensitivity 6-input NCDVI (glmer):** Tier 5 OR = **3.22 (2.64–3.92)**, σ²_u = 0.124, ICC = 0.036. **Endogeneity result holds: 79.6% Tier-5 OR collapse (15.79→3.22).** Invariant across estimators (svyglm 80%, glmer 80%, WeMix scaled 81%).

**Supplementary spec: WeMix::mix PML with PSU-scaled level-1 weights** (Rabe-Hesketh & Skrondal 2006). Tier-5 OR = 27.10 (21.54–34.08), ICC = 0.329. Reported in Supplementary as the fully survey-design-corrected estimate; methods-strict reviewers will expect to see it. Higher conditional OR is consistent with PML theory targeting the population-weighted log-likelihood (see REVAMP_LOG.md Phases 3b–3d for the full attribution).

**Why glmer primary instead of WeMix:** (i) ICC and Tier-5 OR are directly comparable to BDHS HTN literature; (ii) reviewer attention stays on the NCDVI + endogeneity contribution rather than on methodological idiosyncrasy; (iii) full PML estimate available as Supplementary sensitivity for any reviewer who wants it. See REVAMP_LOG.md Phase 3d for the full decision rationale.

## Validation (Phase 4, 2026-05-16, refreshed under 10-input)
TRIPOD-compliant. **AUC (95% CI) for confounders + 10-NCDVI tier: apparent 0.796 (0.787–0.805), 10-fold CV 0.793, bootstrap-corrected 0.794** (B=200, optimism = 0.0022). Δ AUC over confounders-only = 0.053 (p = 4.7e-55). 6-NCDVI Δ AUC = 0.008 (p = 1.9e-06, still significant). Calibration intercept ≈ -0.01, slope ≈ 0.96–0.99 across apparent/CV/bootstrap. Optimism negligible. Well-calibrated, not overfit. See `Figures/phase4_calibration.png`, `outputs/phase4_validation.rds`, `outputs/phase4b_bootstrap.rds`.

## Discrimination enhancement (Phase 4c, 2026-05-16, refreshed under 10-input)
Enhanced spec (M6) reaches **CV AUC = 0.816 (0.807–0.824)**, up from 0.794 in Phase 4. Build-up: continuous NCDVI (+0.013), continuous BMI (+0.006), interactions age:sex and sex:BMI (+0.002), splines (+0.001). **XGBoost on identical features = 0.811** — logistic regression is at the discrimination ceiling on BDHS 2017-18 PR7 variables. Published ML competitors at AUC 0.93+ (Ahmad 2025, Ahmed 2025) must derive gains from variables we don't have or methodological choices (Ahmad: SMOTE + married women only). The interpretable logit captures essentially all available discrimination signal. Keep tier OR table as headline interpretation; use continuous-NCDVI M6 only in the discrimination subsection. See `outputs/phase4c_discrimination_enhance.rds`.
